## Design Context

### Users

**Two audiences, different contexts.**

- **Shelter staff** — often overworked, often on a laptop in a small-town rescue office during the workday. Managing intake, listings, applications, messaging, placements. They need density and findability, not delight. The portal side of the product serves them.
- **Foster parents** — usually on their phone, often late at night or on a weekend. Browsing dogs, considering a foster, writing application notes, messaging with a shelter, rating a completed placement. They need warmth, clarity, and an absence of decision fatigue.

The public marketing pages (`/`, `/terms`, `/privacy`) speak to both — but the foster-parent read is the one that has to land emotionally, because fosters are the scarcer side of the marketplace.

**Job to be done**: "Help me (as a foster or shelter) trust this small platform with a real-world animal placement." Everything subordinates to trust.

### Brand Personality

**Three words**: *pastel, calm, minimal* — with the physical-object mental model of *"a well-designed veterinary pamphlet someone actually wanted to pick up,"* or *"the painted wooden sign above a small-town dog daycare."*

**Emotional goals**:
- First three seconds on any page: *calm* — not excited, not urgent, not upsold.
- Over a full session: *taken seriously, but not intimidated.* This is not a consumer-SaaS funnel. It is animal-welfare work dressed up with care.
- After leaving: *I remember the warmth, not any single feature.*

**Voice / tone in copy**:
- Plain-language, kind, adult. Never cutesy ("pup parent"), never bureaucratic ("application review protocol").
- Short sentences. Resist cleverness. The product itself should read as confident enough not to need clever.
- Numbers are used only when they are true. If we don't have a statistic, we don't fake one.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: *editorial-warm with three soft pastels, applied with real restraint.*

The product lives at the intersection of two reference worlds:

- **Anthropic's minimalism** — palette discipline, serif display moments, animation that responds to user intent instead of autoplaying, trust in whitespace, generous hit-areas, findability over visual drama.
- **A painted dog-daycare signboard** — human-scale warmth, three soft pastels used like signage (each color means something), a handmade steadiness.

The tension between those two is productive. Pure Anthropic alone reads as a research lab; pure daycare alone reads as juvenile. Together they read as *"someone thought hard about this, and they love dogs."*

**References (steal from)**:
- [anthropic.com](https://anthropic.com) — palette restraint, serif-display-for-moments-not-bulk, responsive (never performative) animation, clear navigation, the refusal to show anything unnecessary.

**Anti-references (never feel like)**:
- High-contrast interfaces (black on pure white, cyan on black). Calm contrast only.
- Generic AI / SaaS aesthetic: cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients, gradient text, glassmorphism, sparkline decorations, rounded rectangles with generic drop shadows.
- Over-engineered — too many moving parts, too many sections, too much copy. Information overload.
- Rescue-stock clichés — orange paw prints, saturated red "ADOPT!" buttons, exclamation marks.

**Theme posture**:
- **Light only** on public pages (`/`, `/terms`, `/privacy`). Drop the currently-unused `.dark` tokens from those surfaces.
- **Respect system preference** inside the authenticated portals (`(foster)/` and `(shelter)/` route groups). `next-themes` is already installed — wire it up when the redesign reaches the portal.

**Trust signal strategy** (product-level decision):
- **No placeholder metrics, ever.** No "2,400 dogs fostered" unless that's literally true. No fake 4.9-star ratings. No invented shelter counts.
- How-It-Works *is* the trust signal on the landing page. Explain the flow honestly: what a shelter does, what a foster does, what the platform handles in between. Specificity reads as legitimacy.
- If and when real numbers exist (via analytics integration), we can add them back — but only real ones, and labeled with their source.

## Design Principles

Five principles that every future design decision on this project must satisfy. If a design doesn't pass all five, it is not done.

### 1. Restraint over richness

> "Never over-engineered with too many moving parts."

One meaningful element per moment. If a card has a colored top strip, it doesn't also get a watermark number, a floating icon, and a hover lift — pick one. If a hero has an offset panel, it doesn't also get a grain overlay, a floating rating card, and three gradient washes. Every visual element must be asked "what do you do here?" and unable to answer gets removed.

### 2. Calm contrast

> "Never have a ton of contrast."

No pure black, no pure white. Text is a warm/cool ink (soft navy or warm charcoal) on a cream or bone ground. Borders are whispers (oklch lightness within 6–10 points of the background). Accents are *muted pastels*, not saturated ones. The entire palette stays inside a narrow lightness band with one — and only one — sharper contrast moment per screen (usually the page's primary action).

### 3. Typography leads

> "Similar to a dog daycare" + Anthropic.

Source Serif 4 appears only in **page-level H1s** and at **most one editorial accent** per page (a pull-quote, a marquee number, a section eyebrow). Everywhere else is Switzer — confidently, at weights 400 / 500 / 600. Resist setting every heading in serif. The serif's power is its rarity.

A 1.25 modular scale minimum between type steps. If two sizes look close, the larger one is too small — push it further.

### 4. Three pastels, each with a job

The three accents (butter, sage, peach) are not decoration. Each has a specific product meaning, and that meaning does not move:

| Color | Role | Example usage |
|-------|------|---------------|
| **Butter-yellow** | Brand / foster-side moments / primary CTAs | "I'm a foster parent" button; foster dashboard headers; foster-profile accent |
| **Sage** | Success / placements / verified / completed states | "Placed" status badge; rating success; shelter-verified indicator; positive toasts |
| **Peach** | Shelter-side moments / applications in progress | "I'm a shelter" button; shelter dashboard headers; application-pending states |

If a new accent need arises ("danger" / "urgent"), reach for a muted ink-red *once* and log it here, rather than inventing a fourth brand pastel. The power of these three is that they are rare and meaningful — add a fourth and all four lose their power.

### 5. Motion responds to intent

> "It also has responsive animations which I love."

Motion fires in response to user action, not on autoplay. Page-load fade-ins are OK if they're under ~500ms total, staggered by at most 3 items, and never stack with hover/scroll animations. Any animation longer than 400ms is suspect. `motion-reduce:animate-none` on everything. Animations never delay information — they reveal information that is already there.

No decorative pulses, no idle bounces, no "look at me" loops. If an element is moving when the user isn't doing anything, remove the motion.

## Typography — locked

| Role | Family | Source | Weights |
|------|--------|--------|---------|
| Display | **Source Serif 4** | Google Fonts (`next/font/google`) | 400, 600 |
| Body / UI | **Switzer** | Fontshare (self-hosted via `next/font/local`) | 300, 400, 500, 600, 700 |

**Implementation note**: Switzer is not on Google Fonts. Download the `.woff2` files from [fontshare.com/fonts/switzer](https://www.fontshare.com/fonts/switzer), place them under `src/app/fonts/switzer/`, and load via `next/font/local` in `src/app/layout.tsx`. Keep both families behind CSS variables: `--font-sans` (Switzer) and `--font-display` (Source Serif 4) — the Tailwind config already reads these, so the only touchpoint is `layout.tsx` and the font asset folder.

**Scale** — start with these; refine in the first redesign commit:

```
xs   0.75rem    12px   tiny labels, legal
sm   0.875rem   14px   captions, metadata
base 1rem       16px   body default
lg   1.25rem    20px   lead paragraphs
xl   1.5rem     24px   subheads
2xl  2rem       32px   h2
3xl  2.75rem    44px   h1 on non-landing pages
4xl  3.75rem    60px   landing h1 (serif)
5xl  4.75rem    76px   reserved — use once per product, max
```

At least 1.25× between neighboring steps on the visible page. 1.5× between the page's two most-important sizes.

## Color tokens — locked (OKLCH)

Replace the current HSL tokens in `src/app/globals.css` with these. Keep the same variable names so existing component utilities (`bg-primary`, `text-muted-foreground`, etc.) continue to work — the Tailwind config doesn't need changes.

### Light (the only mode on public pages)

```css
:root {
  /* Surfaces */
  --background:       97.5% 0.010 80;    /* bone */
  --foreground:       26%   0.025 250;   /* soft ink-navy */
  --card:             99%   0.006 80;    /* near-white, warm */
  --card-foreground:  26%   0.025 250;
  --popover:          99%   0.006 80;
  --popover-foreground: 26% 0.025 250;

  /* Neutrals */
  --muted:            95%   0.010 80;
  --muted-foreground: 55%   0.020 250;
  --border:           90%   0.008 80;
  --input:            90%   0.008 80;
  --secondary:        95%   0.010 80;
  --secondary-foreground: 26% 0.025 250;
  --accent:           93%   0.015 80;
  --accent-foreground: 26%  0.025 250;

  /* Three pastels */
  --primary:          85%   0.070 85;    /* butter-yellow (brand / foster / primary CTA) */
  --primary-foreground: 26% 0.025 250;
  --warm:             82%   0.050 155;   /* sage (success / placement / verified) */
  --warm-foreground:  26%   0.025 250;
  --accent-peach:     84%   0.060 35;    /* peach (shelter-side / pending states) */
  --accent-peach-foreground: 26% 0.025 250;

  /* Status */
  --destructive:      62%   0.140 25;    /* muted ink-red, used sparingly */
  --destructive-foreground: 99% 0.006 80;

  --ring:             85%   0.070 85;    /* matches primary */
  --radius:           0.625rem;
}
```

**Important**: because Tailwind `hsl(var(--token))` syntax won't work for OKLCH, update `tailwind.config.ts` to wrap each color token with `oklch(var(--token))` instead of `hsl(var(--token))`. This is a small, mechanical change and should ship in the same commit as the token swap.

### Dark (authenticated portals only)

Derived in the Phase 5-b Commit 6 portal pass. Ships inside the `.dark` class on `<html>` and is only ever mounted by `PortalThemeProvider` (foster + shelter portals). Public pages never see these tokens.

```css
.dark {
  /* Surfaces — warm navy, not black. Lightness stays in a narrow band
     so type-on-surface never reaches pure #000/#fff, mirroring the
     "calm contrast" principle in dark. */
  --background:             20%   0.015 250;   /* deep warm-navy */
  --foreground:             90%   0.010 80;    /* bone on navy */
  --card:                   23%   0.018 250;
  --card-foreground:        90%   0.010 80;
  --popover:                23%   0.018 250;
  --popover-foreground:     90%   0.010 80;

  /* Neutrals */
  --muted:                  26%   0.015 250;
  --muted-foreground:       65%   0.015 80;
  --border:                 30%   0.012 250;
  --input:                  30%   0.012 250;
  --secondary:              26%   0.015 250;
  --secondary-foreground:   90%   0.010 80;
  --accent:                 28%   0.018 250;
  --accent-foreground:      90%   0.010 80;

  /* Three pastels — same hues as light; lightness shifted down ~10
     and chroma up ~3 so they read at dark surface lightness without
     turning saturated. Foreground flips to a deep navy so tinted
     surfaces don't fight the bone body text. */
  --primary:                75%   0.100 85;
  --primary-foreground:     15%   0.020 250;
  --warm:                   72%   0.080 155;
  --warm-foreground:        15%   0.020 250;
  --accent-peach:           74%   0.090 35;
  --accent-peach-foreground: 15%  0.020 250;

  /* Status */
  --destructive:            55%   0.150 25;
  --destructive-foreground: 90%   0.010 80;
  --ring:                   75%   0.100 85;
}
```

These are also mirrored in `src/app/globals.css` so Tailwind can pick them up with the existing `dark:` variant selector. If you edit one side, edit both.

### Removing the old palette

The current HSL tokens (`25 55% 30%` primary warm-brown, `36 90% 55%` honey warm, etc.) get **fully removed** — not coexisted with. Coexistence breeds drift. When the redesign lands, it's a clean token swap in one commit with a screenshot-diff on every touched page.

## What this means for the next commit (landing redesign)

When we pick up the landing page redesign, the implementation has to:

1. **Kill the stats band entirely.** How-It-Works becomes the trust signal.
2. **Drop the floating rating card over the hero photo.** No fake 4.9-star rating.
3. **Swap the fonts** (Source Serif 4 + Switzer) via the layout.tsx change + `src/app/fonts/switzer/` folder.
4. **Swap the palette** (HSL → OKLCH, old tokens → new tokens in `globals.css` + `tailwind.config.ts`).
5. **Restyle the hero** around the new palette + fonts. Offset warm panel stays (becomes sage or peach, picked by role); grain overlay stays but drops to opacity 0.02; eyebrow chip stays but the pulsing dot is removed (no autoplaying motion).
6. **Restyle How-It-Works cards** — each card's top strip picks a single pastel (butter / sage / peach), watermark number stays but drops to `text-foreground/[0.04]`, hover lift stays but shortens to 200ms.
7. **Keep public-footer.tsx** — just restyle against the new palette. Keep Mail-only (no placeholder social).
8. **Apply the migration to /terms, /privacy, and every portal page in the same commit** if any shared component uses the old tokens. Do NOT half-migrate.

We do this as **one commit per logical chunk**, not one massive diff. Probable order: (a) tokens + fonts migration across the whole app; (b) landing hero restyle; (c) landing how-it-works restyle; (d) footer restyle; (e) portal pass. Each a separate commit, each validated with `tsc` + `eslint` + dev smoke.
